Keep your head up, it's just constructive criticism


person: *gives me constructive criticism*
me:


Surely, I am writing this blog with tears in my eyes, lol. After the feedback of demo 1, we had to redo most of our hardcopy maps. Initially, it felt discouraging because of the realisation that we were completely missing the goal. It may seem overwhelming at first but trust in the process. I found constructive criticism to be extremely helpful. My group continuously worked together to assess every output and communicate with the lecturer on what to fix. At the end of demo 2, there was a 20% increase in our marks from demo 1 because we took the constructive feedback into consideration and created outputs that were aligned with it.

My message to you, future GMT 320 Students, is to not be overwhelmed by the criticism. It is there to correct the mistakes you may have overlooked and guide you towards your goal. It is better to rather find out what you did wrong in demo 1 than on the final presentation with external examiners.

Githubbers wishes you all the best!
