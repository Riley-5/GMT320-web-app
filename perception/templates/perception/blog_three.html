Tips For a Successful Project


Here are some of the Project Management Tools to use for a successful project

Work Breakdown Structure:

In project management and systems engineering, a work-breakdown structure (WBS) is a deliverable-oriented breakdown of a project into smaller components. A work breakdown structure is a critical project deliverable that divides the work of the team into manageable chunks. The work-breakdown structure is defined by the Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMBOK 5) as a "hierarchical decomposition of the overall scope of work to be carried out by the project team to achieve the project objectives and create the requisite deliverables."

How it helped our group:

We were able to identify the work that is required or what we must perform, and allocating effort estimates to specific portions of the WBS aided in the rapid construction of a timeline. We utilized it to demonstrate and assign accountabilities and tasks to each team member because it provided a visual of the complete project scope. Furthermore, it served as a tool for group brainstorming and collaboration.

Trello:

Trello is a project management app that divides your projects into boards. Trello tells you what's being worked on, who's working on it, and where something is in the process all at one glance. Consider a white board covered in lists of sticky notes, each one representing a task for you and your team.
Trello adapts to any project, whether you and your team are starting something new or attempting to get more organized with your current work. It intuitively simplifies and standardizes the work process of your team. But don't be fooled by its simplicity! Trello is simple to use while still being capable of handling your team's most complex projects.

Our experiance using Trello:

It is an easy tool to use we did not take time to adapt to the app and be able to use it for our project. It was as simple as it gets, making it ideal for a small group like us seeking for a no-brainer option. Even non-technical users will have no trouble navigating the platform, that’s another advantage of this application. We were also happy to learn that this app has an excellent free plan. Despite its simplicity, we also liked its automation tools as they are generous and just as easy to set up. Trello excels at team collaboration, which is at the heart of project management. It includes a variety of collaboration options, such as comments, document sharing, teammates @mentions, and notifications this helped us a lot in tracking each other’s work progress.

Time Sheet:

A timesheet is a way of keeping track of how much time a worker spends on each job. This information can be recorded on paper or a spreadsheet.

Benefits:

They are helpful in measuring the overall efficiency of your project and reduce time wastage
